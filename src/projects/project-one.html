Currency exchange app

Mobile app | Solo project | 4 weeks | 2025

A currency exchange app that helps travelers understand the true cost of exchanging money by making fees and final amounts visible upfront. The product separates planning and real-time use into two focused experiences, reducing decision friction in unfamiliar environments. Through usability testing, the design evolved to replace empty inputs with meaningful defaults, allowing users to act immediately without hesitation.

Problem: Hidden fees

👉 80% of travelers I interviewed said they double-check exchange rates across multiple apps due to concerns about hidden fees.

Main goal is to help global travelers quickly understand and exchange currencies without cognitive overload, uncertainty, or hidden friction.

Solution: Two core experiences

Design an app includes two core experiences: Convert and Calculate. Convert for preparing for a trip abroad and Calculate for on-location calculations.

Key Decision: Default inputs

👉 Use default inputs over guidance indicators.

In the first and second round of testing all users paused at currency input task. Competitor analysis informed my decision to switch generic icons to default currencies. The users no longer paused at the same task during the final round of testing.

Research

Interviews: Clarity is important to the user

Examples of questions I asked
  • "How do you usually plan and prepare for managing money before a trip?"
  • "Where do you typically exchange currency and how do you decide which place to use?"
  • "What tools do you use to check exchange rates or fees?"
Trends: trust depends on full disclosure

Across interviews I found that users don’t trust exchange rates on their own. They look for the full picture (fees, final amount, and clear breakdowns) before making a decision. Without that, they cross-check multiple sources, trying to confirm whether they’re getting a fair deal, often struggling to interpret what the numbers actually mean.

Competitor Analysis

Analysis of Wise and XE shows that while both provide reliable exchange rates, neither clearly presents the full cost upfront. Fees are hidden, delayed, or require sign-up, and key actions are interrupted by cluttered or limited interfaces.

Design

Sketches: Pop-up ideation

Started with rapid hand-drawn sketches with an early idea of using pop up for information like Mid-Makret rate.

Wireframes: Design for trust

Early wireframes included a “Paying with” step and a separate Favorites flow. These features added extra decisions without helping users understand the final amount or build trust. In the final design, I removed them to keep the experience focused on rates, and detailed transfer methods.

Designing the rate and fee flow focused on reducing unnecessary steps. Early versions split actions across multiple buttons, bringing users back to the main screen multiple times before completing the calculation. The final design combines both into a single entry point and moves input to a dedicated screen, allowing users to complete the task in one step.

Usability Study: Currency inputs are unclear

I tested interactive wireframe prototypes with four participants across two rounds to validate core flows before moving to visual design.

Test tasks
  • Convert $1,000 USD to NZD.
  • Find out what mid-market rate is.
  • Use calculator to manually enter 1.74 exchange rate and $5 service fee.
Findings

Users ignored the progress bar and hesitated at unclear inputs. Currency selection was not immediately understood, but consistently treated as the first logical step before entering an amount.

Final

Explore Interactive Prototype →

The final design made exchange costs easy to understand by clearly separating the mid-market rate from the final amount after fees. Pre-filled currencies and amounts, along with ranking options by total cost, helped users act with confidence. A transparent fee breakdown reinforced trust and reduced the need to double-check across multiple sources.
