Project Overview

Camelot Vet Services

Project Overview

Website | Collaborative project | Ongoing | 2026

Led strategy, UX direction, and 10DLC compliance for a small veterinary practice launching its first professional website — collaborating with a web designer to deliver a mobile-friendly, visually consistent Squarespace site across four pages.

Problem

Camelot Vet Clinic, a long-established private practice in Eantown, PA, had no active website and no formal digital presence. The immediate trigger was compliance: the clinic was onboarding to a 10DLC texting system — a carrier-level regulation requiring businesses to register their messaging campaigns — and that registration requires a live, compliant website to be approved. Without it, the clinic could not reach patients by text at all.

👉 Patients can't be reached by text until a live compliance site exists.

Beyond the regulatory urgency, the clinic had a deeper discoverability problem. Their Google Business profile — carrying 4.7 stars and 252 reviews — was unclaimed. New patients couldn't verify the practice online, and the clinic had no way to convert that trust into appointments.

Solution

A simple, fast, and low-maintenance website built on Squarespace — designed to go live within a week and serve as the foundation for future growth. The site covers four pages: Home, About, Services, and Terms & Conditions (required for 10DLC compliance).

The design priority was clarity and consistency: a mobile-first layout that reflects the warmth and personal character of the clinic, with a visual system that holds together across all pages without requiring ongoing maintenance from the client.

Process

Led strategy, compliance guidance, and design direction — working collaboratively with a web designer to move from a cold discovery call to a near-complete site in under two weeks.

Discovery

Discovery Call

Joined the initial client call to understand the clinic's needs, constraints, and goals. The client — the Office Manager of a small, privately owned practice — was clear: she wanted something fast, simple, and low-maintenance. She didn't have a logo or polished brand assets, just a Google Business profile, a few old ads, and a phone number.

Key information gathered during the call:

  • Services offered: wellness exams, vaccinations, digital X-ray, dentals, soft tissue surgeries, emergency care, and care for cats, dogs, and small exotics
  • Most important service: surgery
  • Differentiator: small, private practice with a personalized approach — patients travel from West Virginia, Pittsburgh, and Ohio specifically to see Dr. Shepherd
  • Design preference: simple and clean
  • No existing website; domain to be purchased through Squarespace

I also flagged the Google Business profile issue during the call and pushed for the client to re-attempt claiming it — the 4.7-star rating and existing review volume were too valuable to leave unmanaged.

Strategy

Compliance First

The 10DLC registration process requires a publicly accessible website that includes, at minimum, a Terms & Conditions page with explicit language about SMS messaging consent and opt-out instructions. This wasn't optional — without it, the carrier registration would be rejected and the clinic would remain unable to contact patients by text.

I structured the site scope around this requirement: four pages that satisfy both the compliance requirement and the client's real-world need for a professional, informative web presence.

Scope Definition

Defined a focused MVP rather than a feature-heavy launch. The first version intentionally excludes online booking and click-to-text functionality — not because those aren't valuable, but because they introduce complexity that would delay the go-live date and require ongoing client involvement to maintain. A lean, accurate, compliant site that goes live this week is more useful than a feature-rich site that launches in a month.

Page structure:

  • Home — Overview of the clinic, services summary, and contact information
  • About — Short introduction to Dr. Shepherd and the clinic's approach
  • Services — Organized list of what the clinic offers
  • Terms & Conditions — 10DLC-compliant SMS consent and privacy language

Design Direction

Visual Consistency Across Pages

After the web designer built the initial Squarespace structure, I spent a focused working session reviewing the site end-to-end — going through colors, imagery, spacing between elements, call-to-action behavior, and how the design held together across all four pages on mobile.

The guiding principle was consistency: a visitor landing on the Services page should feel the same visual language as one landing on the Home page. Small practices often end up with sites that feel assembled rather than designed — different font weights on different pages, inconsistent button styles, imagery that doesn't match the tone. My direction was to close those gaps before launch.

Mobile-First Review

The client's patient base spans a wide age range, and many will find the site by searching on a phone. I prioritized the mobile experience throughout the design review — checking that CTAs were tappable, contact information was immediately visible, and the layout didn't break on smaller screens. Desktop was secondary.

Results

Key Product Decisions

Decision 1: Lead with compliance, not aesthetics

The temptation with any client who has no existing site is to treat it as a blank-canvas design opportunity. But the real constraint here was regulatory — the site needed to exist and be compliant before anything else mattered.

Framing the project around the 10DLC deadline gave the work a clear, non-negotiable go-live target and prevented scope creep from features that sounded nice but weren't required. It also helped the client understand why certain pages — especially Terms & Conditions — weren't optional add-ons but load-bearing parts of the build.

Decision 2: Preserve the clinic's existing trust signals

The clinic's strongest asset wasn't its brand — it was its reputation. Dr. Shepherd is, by the client's own description, something of a local icon. Patients drive from neighboring states specifically to see him. The Google Business profile, even unclaimed, carried 4.7 stars across 252 reviews with consistent mentions of him and the office manager by name.

Rather than treating the website as the primary trust-builder, I directed the content and design to reinforce what already existed: a warm, personalized practice with a doctor patients are loyal to. The About page and homepage copy were shaped around that character — not generic clinic language, but the specificity that makes a small practice feel worth driving two hours to reach.

Project Successes

  • Moved from zero web presence to a near-complete, compliant website in under two weeks
  • 10DLC Terms & Conditions page structured and in place ahead of carrier registration
  • Consistent visual system across all four pages, optimized for mobile
  • Content strategy grounded in the clinic's real differentiator — its people — rather than generic veterinary copy
  • Scope held to MVP, keeping the path to launch fast and the maintenance burden low for the client

What I Learned

Not every website project starts with a design brief — sometimes it starts with a regulatory deadline and a client who feeds tigers after lunch.

Working within a real business constraint (10DLC compliance) sharpened every decision. There was no room to over-design or over-scope, and that pressure produced a cleaner, more purposeful outcome than an open-ended brief often does. The strongest design direction came not from inventing a brand identity but from listening carefully in a 20-minute discovery call and building around what was already true about the practice.

What's Next

Once the site is live and the 10DLC registration is approved, the foundation is in place for incremental improvements: online appointment requests, a click-to-text button, and — when the client is ready — a separate site for the wild animal orphanage she manages, which will need donation functionality and its own identity.
