Camelot Vet Services

Project Overview

Website | Collaborative project | Ongoing | 2026

Led strategy, UX direction, and 10DLC compliance for a small veterinary practice rebuilding its digital presence, collaborating with a web designer to deliver a mobile-friendly and visually consistent Squarespace site across four pages.

Problem

Camelot Vet Services, a long-established private practice in Eantown, PA, lost its website and digital infrastructure years prior. When the clinic onboarded to Weave for patient communication, they ran into a wall. 10DLC carrier registration requires that a business has

  • a live, compliant website
  • SMS consent language on that website

Without both, the clinic could not reach patients by text at all.

👉 Patients can't be reached by text until a live compliance site exists.

Beyond the regulatory urgency, the clinic had a deeper discoverability problem. They had an unclaimed Google Business profile with 4.7 stars and 252 reviews sitting there, unmanaged and disconnected from any website.

Solution

A simple, fast, and low-maintenance website built on Squarespace, designed to go live within a week and serve as the foundation for future growth. The site covers four pages that include Home, About, Services, and Terms and Conditions required for 10DLC compliance.

The design priority was clarity and consistency: a mobile-first layout that highlights the warmth and personal character of the clinic, with a visual system that holds together across all pages without requiring ongoing maintenance from the client.

Process

Led strategy, compliance guidance, and design direction, working collaboratively with a web designer to move from a cold discovery call to a near-complete site in under two weeks.

Discovery

Discovery Call

Joined the initial client call to understand the clinic's needs, constraints, and goals. The client, the Office Manager of a small privately owned practice, was clear that she wanted something fast, simple, and low-maintenance. She didn't have a logo or polished brand assets, just a Google Business profile, a few old ads, and a phone number.

Key information gathered during the call:

  • Services offered: wellness exams, vaccinations, digital X-ray, dentals, soft tissue surgeries, emergency care, and care for cats, dogs, and small exotics
  • Most important service: surgery
  • Differentiator: small, private practice with a personalized approach. Patients travel from West Virginia, Pittsburgh, and Ohio specifically to see Dr. Shepherd
  • Design preference: simple and clean
  • No existing website; domain to be purchased through Squarespace

I also flagged the Google Business profile issue during the call and pushed for the client to re-attempt claiming it. The 4.7-star rating and existing review volume were too valuable to leave unmanaged.

Strategy

Compliance First

The 10DLC registration process requires a publicly accessible website that includes, at minimum, a Terms & Conditions page with explicit language about SMS messaging consent and opt-out instructions. Without this page, the carrier registration would be rejected and the clinic would remain unable to contact patients by text.

I structured the site scope around this requirement: four pages that satisfy both the compliance requirement and the client's real-world need for a professional, informative web presence.

Scope Definition

Defined a focused MVP rather than a feature-heavy launch. The first version intentionally excludes online booking and click-to-text functionality because they introduce complexity that would delay the go-live date and require ongoing client involvement to maintain.

Page structure:

  • Home — Overview of the clinic, services summary, and contact information
  • About — Introduction to Dr. Shepherd and the clinic's approach
  • Services — Organized list of what the clinic offers
  • Terms & Conditions — 10DLC-compliant SMS consent and privacy language

Design Direction

Visual Consistency Across Pages

After the web designer built the initial Squarespace structure, I spent a focused working session reviewing the site end to end: colors, imagery, spacing between elements, call-to-action behavior, and how the design held together across all four pages on mobile.

The guiding principle was consistency: a visitor landing on the Services page should feel the same visual language as one landing on the Home page. Small practices often end up with sites that feel assembled rather than designed. Different font weights on different pages, inconsistent button styles, imagery that doesn't match the tone. My direction was to close those gaps before launch.

Results

Key Product Decisions

Decision 1: Lead with compliance, not aesthetics

The site needed to exist before anything else could happen. The clinic could not use texting until a compliant website was live. That requirement set the real timeline for the project.

Because of that, the goal was not to design the most elaborate site possible. The goal was to launch a simple and compliant version quickly. Focusing on that kept the scope small and helped the client understand why pages like Terms and Conditions were necessary from the start.

Decision 2: Preserve the clinic's existing trust signals

The clinic already had a strong reputation in the community. Dr. Shepherd is well known locally and patients are willing to travel long distances to see him. Their Google listing also showed 4.7 stars across more than two hundred reviews, with many people mentioning the doctor and the office staff by name.

Instead of trying to invent a new brand story, the website reinforces that existing trust. The content highlights the clinic as a small and personal practice where patients feel known. The goal was to reflect the experience people already talk about in reviews.

Project Successes

  • Moved from zero web presence to a near-complete, compliant website in under two weeks
  • 10DLC Terms & Conditions page structured and in place ahead of carrier registration
  • Consistent visual system across all four pages, optimized for mobile
  • Content strategy grounded in the clinic's real differentiator, its people, rather than generic veterinary copy
  • Scope held to MVP, keeping the path to launch fast and the maintenance burden low for the client

What I Learned

Not every website project starts with a design brief. Sometimes it starts with a regulatory deadline and a client who feeds tigers after lunch.

Working within a real business constraint (10DLC compliance) sharpened every decision. There was no room to over-design or over-scope, and that pressure produced a cleaner, more purposeful outcome than an open-ended brief often does. The strongest design direction came not from inventing a brand identity but from listening carefully in a 20-minute discovery call and building around what was already true about the practice.

What's Next

With the site live and 10DLC registration underway, the foundation is in place for incremental improvements: online appointment requests, a click-to-text button, and a separate site for the wild animal orphanage the client manages, which will need donation functionality and its own identity.
