Chani

Mobile app | Solo project | 2 weeks | 2025

A feature addition to the Chani app that lets users save and quickly return to favorite Meditations, Affirmations, and Sleep Stories. The goal was to fix the drop-off after listening, where users couldn’t easily find content they loved again. By combining in-place saving with a restructured Listen page, the experience shifted from searching and scrolling to immediate retrieval.

Problem

👉 I can’t find the meditation I liked yesterday without scrolling and trying to recognize it from memory.

Finding a meditation I liked later took effort. I had to scroll through multiple categories and try to recognize it from memory, or rely on screenshots to find it again.

Solution

Add an outlined heart icon on the Play screen to signal saving. Organize the Listen page using category tabs and push saved content to the top of each list.

Strategy

Decision: Solve for retrieval, not just saving

👉 Consider how to find a saved file later.

Saving alone wouldn’t fix the problem. The experience needed to make previously favorited content immediately visible without requiring users to search again.

Constraint: Work within an existing pattern

The solution had to fit into Chani’s current UI. I extended the tab pattern from the Home page to organize the Listen categories, keeping the experience familiar while supporting new functionality.

Research: What breaks after listening

Creating a user journey helped me realize that the critical task was finding the meditation I liked the next day.

👉 Manually scroll through all three categories to find one specific file.

The app encourages meditation, but returning to a saved experience requires scanning, memory, or workarounds like screenshots. I focused on reducing effort after the initial interaction.

Compare Approach

Two solutions to the same problem, starting from different places — I extended patterns across the app, while Chani restructured the Listen page as its own system.

My approach: Retrieval first

I started from the Audio Player screen and used a familiar tab pattern to organize the Listen page. This made it possible to add a full-width search input, keep the page description easy to scan, and place saved items at the top without scrolling.

Chani’s approach: Saving first

It appears Chani started from the Listen page and reorganized it as its own system. This made it possible to introduce search and filter, add a favorites section, group content into categories, and mark saved items with a heart icon within the library.

Design: Wireframes

With a clear problem and an existing design system to work within, I moved directly to wireframes in Figma. Four screens capture the core of the solution: the Home page establishing the tab pattern I would carry into the Listen page, the Play screen before and after saving a file, and the redesigned Listen page showing category tabs with a favorited audio file surfaced at the top.
