About Me...

My path to UX design began with curiosity, sparked by an early introduction to user experience research during my university years. That interest led me to earn a degree in Web Design and Development in 2019 and a lasting focus on thoughtful, people-centered digital experiences. I’ve since worked as a freelance designer and as an onboarding project manager at a mid-sized tech company, supporting real customers as they navigated complex products. Those experiences shape how I design today: with insight, empathy, and a deep respect for how people actually work.

I design human-centered UX for products people rely on every day. Drawing on experience supporting and onboarding real customers, I focus on clarity, usability, and long-term adoption.

Think

I journal daily as a way to reflect, notice patterns, and make sense of complex ideas. I started journaling at ten, and it has remained my most reliable way of processing information over time. Writing helps me slow down, connect dots, and turn experience into understanding.

Connect

Outside of work, I’m drawn to projects that turn individual effort into shared benefit. Recently, I started a small composting system in my apartment building after realizing I didn’t generate enough food waste on my own. I partnered with a restaurant downstairs to collect their scraps, manage a weekly exchange, and process everything through my home compost system. The finished compost goes back into the community—shared with friends, family, and neighbors who grow food or tend gardens.

Grow

In 2026, I want to explore woodworking, as learning a tactile craft feels like a natural extension of how I think about design. My grandfather was a brilliant furniture-maker, known for pieces with intricate carved details, and I like to think some of that curiosity for craft lives on in me. More than anything, this goal is about stepping into creative spaces with openness, trying things that once felt out of reach and letting new skills shape how I see and make.
